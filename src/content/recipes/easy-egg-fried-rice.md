ingredients:
  - "4 cups cold cooked rice (day-old works best)"
  - "3 eggs, beaten"
  - "2 tbsp oil"
  - "1 cup frozen peas and carrots"
  - "3 green onions, sliced (or 1/2 small onion)"
  - "3 tbsp soy sauce"
  - "1 tsp sesame oil (optional but lovely)"
instructions:
  - "Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble until just set, then scoop out and set aside."
  - "Add the remaining oil and the frozen peas and carrots. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes."
  - "Add the cold rice, breaking up any clumps, and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until heated through and slightly toasted."
  - "Pour in the soy sauce and toss to coat. Stir the eggs back in along with the green onions and sesame oil."
  - "Toss everything together for another minute and serve hot."
notes: "Cold, day-old rice is the secret — fresh rice turns mushy. This is the ultimate 'clean out the fridge' meal; toss in any leftover veggies or protein."
