Breakfast doesn't have to mean expensive cereal or a costly coffee-shop run. With a few cheap staples — oats, eggs, flour, a couple of bananas — you can feed the whole family a filling morning meal for next to nothing. Here are three of our favorite **cheap breakfast ideas**. ☀️

## 1. [Easy Overnight Oats (4 Ways)](/recipes/overnight-oats/) — ~$0.50/jar

The ultimate make-ahead breakfast. Stir together in 5 minutes tonight, grab a ready jar tomorrow. Four cheap flavor combos so you never get bored.

## 2. [Fluffy Buttermilk Pancakes from Scratch](/recipes/fluffy-buttermilk-pancakes/) — ~$0.50/serving

Tastier *and* cheaper than a boxed mix, using ingredients you already have. Make a double batch and freeze extras for instant weekday breakfasts.

## 3. [Banana Oat Breakfast Muffins](/recipes/banana-oat-muffins/) — ~$0.25/muffin

The best way to rescue those spotty bananas no one will eat. A dozen wholesome, grab-and-go muffins for about $3.

## Granny's cheap-breakfast tips

- **Buy oats in the big bag.** Rolled oats are one of the cheapest, most filling foods you can buy — and they work in oats, muffins, and pancakes alike.
- **Freeze in portions.** Muffins and pancakes freeze beautifully; reheat straight from frozen on busy mornings.
- **Eggs stretch everything.** A couple of eggs turn cheap staples into a filling, protein-rich meal.
