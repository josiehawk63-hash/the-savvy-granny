Few things stretch a food budget like a big pot of soup. A bag of dried lentils, a few cans of tomatoes, some cheap beans — and suddenly you've got dinner *and* a stack of freezer lunches. Here are our coziest, cheapest **budget soups and chili**. 🍲

## 1. [Hearty Lentil Soup](/recipes/hearty-lentil-soup/) — ~$1/serving

Protein-packed and filling, made with some of the cheapest ingredients in the store. Freezes beautifully and tastes even better the next day.

## 2. [Creamy Tomato Soup from Pantry Staples](/recipes/creamy-tomato-soup/) — ~$1.25/serving

Velvety and restaurant-worthy from humble canned tomatoes. Pair it with a grilled cheese for the ultimate cheap comfort meal.

## 3. [Easy Slow Cooker Chili](/recipes/slow-cooker-chili/) — ~$1.25/serving

Dump everything in the crockpot and let it work its magic. One big batch covers dinner, leftovers, and the freezer.

## Granny's tips for cheap, hearty soups

- **Beans and lentils are your best friends.** Dried beans cost pennies per serving and add protein, fiber, and staying power.
- **Always make a double batch.** Soup freezes perfectly — portion it into containers for ready-made lunches.
- **Stale bread = free croutons.** Cube it, toss with a little oil, and toast. Nothing goes to waste.
