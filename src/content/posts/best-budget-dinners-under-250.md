## Granny's tips for stretching your dinner budget

- **Buy dried, not canned, when you can.** Dried beans, lentils, and rice cost a fraction of canned and store for ages.
- **Build meals around what's on sale.** Plan your week *after* checking the markdowns, not before.
- **Cook once, eat twice.** Almost everything here doubles and freezes beautifully.
- **A well-stocked pantry is a money-saver.** Keeping a few cheap staples on hand means you can always pull together a meal instead of ordering takeout.

### A few budget-kitchen tools I swear by

You don't need fancy gear to cook cheap, delicious food — but a few sturdy basics make it easier and last for years:

- A **heavy-bottom pot or Dutch oven** for soups and big batches
- A **good sheet pan** that won't warp
- A **cast iron skillet** that does everything and lasts a lifetime
